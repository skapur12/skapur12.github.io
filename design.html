ApplyBug

My brother, currently, a senior in high school, is applying to colleges, and he once complained to me that it was difficult to keep track of all of the requirements for each college. I decided to help him out by compiling all of the required information for undergraduate admission for most colleges in North Carolina (since I’m from there) onto one website.

Designed and created using Squarespace, each page includes information about essays, letters of recommendation, standardized testing, and much more!

Travel Troopers

During the summer of 2018, I studied abroad in Barcelona, Spain. While there, I took a course entitled Human-Computer Interaction: User Interface Design. For our class project, my team and I designed prototypes of a mobile application and an augmented reality flexible map interface.

These two interfaces helped groups traveling abroad locate restaurants based on recommendations from their friends, provided directions to unfamiliar locations, and allowed people to chat and share photos from their trips. In essence, it is sort of like an integration of the functionalities of Yelp Reviews and Slack Chat and Snapchat maps.

Pothole Reporting System

I designed a mobile application for a pothole reporting system. Using this app, cities can efficiently repair potholes by prioritizing the larger ones, the older ones, the ones on busy roads, the more dangerous ones, etc. for repair.

The city can then schedule a repair crew to take care of the pothole as quickly as possible. People can also take report potholes by photographing them and uploading them to the app, or shouting “Pothole!” once they hit one on a road, so that the location of the pothole can be tracked. The repair statuses of potholes around the city can be viewed on the app as well.
